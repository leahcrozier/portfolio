Hi! I'm Leah :)
Welcome to my Portfolio

Hello! I'm a 17 year old British gal currently residing in the Netherlands. I've been interested in art, design, the internet and computers since I was little. For the past 6 years I've been part of the Coderclass, a section within the Metis Montessori Lyceum where I have worked on various projects, from a light-up skirt to a living room in VR. Some of these projects are listed below, along with other things I have made.


My main skills lie in visual programs, such as After Effects, Blender and Photoshop. All of which I have been using regularly throughout the years and have gotten comfortable with. Besides that, I have some basic knowledge on coding languages such as HTML, CSS, Javascript, Python and a little bit of R and C#.

3D Printed Angel Statue


A 3D printed "statue" made as part of a large project (profielwerkstuk) on the definitions of digital art. The model itself was made in Blender (slides 2-5), sliced in Prusa Slicer, and printed on a Prusa i3 MK3S. This was my first real attempt at 3D printing, so the model itself is slightly messy. If I were to print it again, I would probably play around with the layer heights to try and make it look a bit cleaner.

Fury Fly in the Witches' Kitchen


A multiplayer (2 player) platformer where two chubby flies try their best to be the last to survive in the Witches' kitchen, where the water rises after a while. This game was made during an 8-hour Game Jam, together with Jochem Baas, Luuk Noverraz, and Codemaker_4. The theme of the Game Jam was "30 seconds", so each match has a 30-second timer. If you would like to play it, you can do so here.

Unity VR Room


This living room/kitchen was made as a Unity VR project. Small items, such as plates, cups and books can be picked up and thrown, and light switches are able to be interacted with as well. All of the models used were created in Blender and imported into Unity, so it can be viewed in VR.

Escape Room


For a project, Divya and myself transformed a classroom into an Escape room. There were a variety of puzzles, including a Blender "Game", where the player had to walk through a recreated version of the classroom they were in to find clues, to then be able to locate parts of a code in the real world. To escape, they needed to enter the correct 4-number code into a Micro:Bit lock (slide 5)
